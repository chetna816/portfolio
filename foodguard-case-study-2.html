FoodGuard

An AI-enabled food donation platform connecting donors, volunteers, and NGOs — turning surplus food into meals for those who need them most.

Aligned with 🌾 SDG 2 — Zero Hunger 🏙 SDG 11 — Sustainable Cities ♻️ SDG 12 — Responsible Consumption

The Problem

Food is wasted. People go hungry.

Every day, restaurants, events, and households discard massive quantities of surplus food — while millions of people across communities face food insecurity. These two realities exist side by side, disconnected simply because there's no efficient system to bridge them.

Existing donation efforts are fragmented, manual, and hard to coordinate. Donors don't know who needs food. NGOs struggle to manage pickups. Volunteers have no visibility. The result: food that could feed people ends up in landfills.

"What if surplus food from a restaurant could reach a family in need — in real time, with zero friction?"

Design Process

How I approached it

FoodGuard needed to serve four very different user types — donors, volunteers, NGOs, and administrators — each with their own needs and levels of tech-literacy. The design process was driven by one question: how do we make doing good feel effortless?

01
Research & Problem Definition
Studied the landscape of food waste and hunger — including SDG frameworks and existing donation systems. Identified the core gap: lack of real-time coordination between donors, NGOs, and volunteers. Mapped out all four user roles and their specific pain points.
02
User Flows & Information Architecture
Designed separate flows for each user type — a donor listing surplus food, a volunteer accepting a pickup, an NGO tracking distributions, and an admin overseeing the entire system. Kept each flow to the minimum steps needed so nothing slows down the process.
03
UI Design & Prototyping
Designed a warm, approachable visual system — friendly enough for everyday users, clear enough for coordination at scale. Used colour coding to distinguish roles and real-time status indicators for pickups and deliveries. Built interactive prototypes to test the donation-to-delivery journey end to end.
04
Tech Integration
Collaborated on integrating the UI with a Node.js backend and MongoDB database — enabling real-time tracking, volunteer assignment, and distribution monitoring. Designed the interface to surface live data intuitively so every stakeholder always knows the status of a donation.

Key Features

What FoodGuard does

The platform is built around a centralised digital system with four interconnected modules — each designed for a specific role in the food donation chain.

🍱
Donor Module
Restaurants, households, and event organisers can list surplus food in seconds — specifying type, quantity, and pickup window.
🚴
Volunteer Assignment
Nearby volunteers are matched to pickups automatically. Real-time tracking keeps donors and NGOs updated throughout.
🤝
NGO Dashboard
NGOs get full visibility into incoming donations, volunteer activity, and distribution progress — all in one place.
📊
Admin & Analytics
Administrators monitor the entire ecosystem — tracking food saved, meals delivered, and system performance with full transparency.
